Recipes Tacos

Description

French tacos are a street dish originally from Lyon, France, that combines international influences. Despite their name, they don't have much in common with Mexican tacos. They are prepared by wrapping meat (such as chicken, beef or kebab), fries, cheese and sauces in a burrito-type wheat tortilla, which is then folded into a rectangular shape and heated on a griddle or press until golden and crispy. It is a fast and forceful meal, popular among students and young people, and is usually offered with a variety of combinations and personalized sauces.

Ingredients

  • Wheat tortilla
  • Meat (Chicken, beef, kebab or falafel)
  • French Fries
  • Cheese
  • Vegetables (Lettuce, tomato, onion)
  • White sauce (Aioli, garlic cream, mayonnaise)
  • Spicy or tomato-based (Ketchup, harissa)
  • Cheese sauce (Optional)

Steps

  1. Cook the meat of your choice (chicken, beef, kebab, or falafel) and season to taste.
  2. Fry the French fries until golden and crispy.
  3. Warm a wheat tortilla in a pan to make it more flexible.
  4. Place the meat in the center of the tortilla.
  5. Add the French fries on top of the meat.
  6. Sprinkle cheese (shredded or melted) over the filling.
  7. Add optional vegetables (lettuce, tomato, onion).
  8. Drizzle the sauces (white sauce, spicy, or your favorite) over the filling.
  9. Fold the tortilla into a rectangular shape, making sure it's well sealed.
  10. Cook the taco in a press or pan over medium-high heat until golden and crispy on both sides.
  11. Remove from heat, cut if desired, and serve hot.
